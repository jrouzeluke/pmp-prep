# PMP Exam Bank 1: People - Managing Conflict
# ECO Alignment: Domain I, Task 1

## Question 1
Two senior developers on an agile team are in a heated debate over the technical architecture of a new feature. The disagreement is beginning to affect the sprint velocity. What should the project manager do first?
- [x] Facilitate a meeting between the two developers to help them reach a consensus.
- [ ] Make a final decision on the architecture to ensure the sprint stays on track.
- [ ] Escalate the issue to the functional manager to decide on the best technical approach.
- [ ] Separate the developers by assigning them to different tasks for the remainder of the sprint.
**Explanation:** In agile, the PM (or Scrum Master) should facilitate communication and empower the team to resolve technical conflicts internally before intervening authoritatively.

## Question 2
A project manager is overseeing a global project. Two stakeholders from different regions have conflicting requirements for a key deliverable. The project manager realizes this is due to a cultural misunderstanding. What is the best approach?
- [x] Identify the source of the conflict and facilitate a discussion to find a win-win solution.
- [ ] Request that the project sponsor decide which regional requirement takes precedence.
- [ ] Adhere strictly to the initial project charter and reject any requirements that deviate.
- [ ] Ignore the conflict and hope the stakeholders resolve it during the next status meeting.
**Explanation:** Collaborating/Problem Solving is the most effective way to address root causes and ensure long-term stakeholder satisfaction.

## Question 3
During a project meeting, a team member continuously interrupts others and dismisses their ideas, causing visible tension. Which conflict management style is the team member exhibiting?
- [ ] Compromising
- [x] Forcing/Competing
- [ ] Smoothing/Accommodating
- [ ] Collaborating
**Explanation:** Forcing involves pushing one's own viewpoint at the expense of others, often resulting in a win-lose situation.

## Question 4
A project manager decides to delay testing by two days to satisfy a production manager’s immediate need, even though it increases the risk of late defects. Which technique was used?
- [ ] Withdrawal
- [x] Smoothing/Accommodating
- [ ] Problem Solving
- [ ] Compromising
**Explanation:** Smoothing emphasizes areas of agreement or yields to the position of others to maintain harmony or meet an immediate need.

## Question 5
In an agile environment, the Product Owner and the Team disagree on the definition of 'Done'. How should the Scrum Master handle this?
- [ ] Update the Definition of Done based on personal experience and inform the team.
- [x] Facilitate a discussion during the next retrospective to reach a shared understanding.
- [ ] Tell the team to follow the Product Owner’s instructions.
- [ ] Ask the Project Sponsor to mediate the dispute.
**Explanation:** Agile conflicts regarding process are best handled through collaborative facilitation and shared team agreements.

## Question 6
A PM notices two team members have stopped speaking after a disagreement. The PM decides to wait and see if they work it out. Which technique is this?
- [ ] Compromising
- [ ] Collaborating
- [x] Avoiding/Withdrawing
- [ ] Forcing
**Explanation:** Avoiding involves retreating from a conflict or postponing the issue to a later time.

## Question 7
You meet with a chronically late team member privately to discuss the impact of their delays and listen to their challenges. This is:
- [ ] Forcing a resolution
- [x] Collaborative problem-solving
- [ ] Smoothing the conflict
- [ ] Avoiding the issue
**Explanation:** Addressing root causes through direct, private communication is a hallmark of the problem-solving approach.

## Question 8
Two functional managers want the same resource. What should the PM do?
- [ ] Split the resource 50/50 between both tasks.
- [x] Analyze project priorities and discuss needs with managers to find an optimal schedule.
- [ ] Escalate to the PMO immediately.
- [ ] Tell the resource to decide which project they prefer.
**Explanation:** Using data and priorities to facilitate a resolution is the most professional and effective approach.

## Question 9
Which is a positive outcome of conflict if managed correctly?
- [ ] Increased groupthink
- [x] Improved creativity and better decision making
- [ ] A clearer hierarchy of power
- [ ] Reduced project documentation
**Explanation:** Constructive conflict allows different perspectives to be aired, leading to more innovative solutions.

## Question 10
A PM asks two parties to each give up something to reach a temporary solution. Which technique is this?
- [ ] Collaborating
- [x] Compromising
- [ ] Forcing
- [ ] Avoiding
**Explanation:** Compromising involves finding a middle ground where both parties concede certain points.

## Question 11
A team is in the "Storming" phase of development. Conflicts over roles and responsibilities are frequent. What should the PM do?
- [ ] Reassign the team members to different projects.
- [x] Guidance the team in establishing clear ground rules and role definitions.
- [ ] Allow the team to fail so they learn from the experience.
- [ ] Step back and let the team self-organize without any intervention.
**Explanation:** During Storming, the PM should provide high levels of guidance to help the team establish norms.

## Question 12
A stakeholder is unhappy with the project's progress and expresses this aggressively in a meeting. The PM acknowledges the stakeholder's concerns but refocuses the group on the items they currently agree on. This is:
- [ ] Forcing
- [ ] Compromising
- [x] Smoothing
- [ ] Collaborating
**Explanation:** Smoothing downplays the differences and emphasizes areas of agreement.

## Question 13
Two team members are arguing over which software tool to use. The PM directs them to use Tool A because it is the company standard, despite Tool B having better features for this specific task. This is:
- [ ] Problem Solving
- [x] Forcing
- [ ] Withdrawal
- [ ] Compromise
**Explanation:** Using authority or a mandate to resolve a conflict is Forcing.

## Question 14
Which conflict resolution technique is most likely to result in a permanent solution?
- [ ] Smoothing
- [ ] Compromising
- [ ] Forcing
- [x] Collaborating
**Explanation:** Collaboration addresses the root cause through consensus, making the solution more sustainable.

## Question 15
Conflict is most often caused by which of the following in a project environment?
- [ ] Personalities
- [ ] Cost
- [x] Schedules
- [ ] Management styles
**Explanation:** Statistically, schedule priorities are the most common source of conflict in projects.

## Question 16
An agile team is experiencing internal friction regarding who should perform testing. The PM suggests they try a "pair testing" approach for one sprint to see if it helps. This is:
- [x] Collaborative problem solving
- [ ] Forcing
- [ ] Avoiding
- [ ] Smoothing
**Explanation:** Experimenting with a solution based on team input is a collaborative way to solve a process problem.

## Question 17
A project manager is mediating a conflict. They ask each person to describe the other person's perspective. This is a technique to build:
- [ ] Authority
- [x] Empathy and understanding
- [ ] A paper trail
- [ ] A reason to terminate a member
**Explanation:** Active listening and perspective-sharing are key to resolving interpersonal conflicts.

## Question 18
When is "Avoiding" an appropriate conflict management strategy?
- [x] When the issue is trivial or more important issues are pressing.
- [ ] When you need a permanent solution.
- [ ] When you have more power than the other party.
- [ ] When the project is behind schedule.
**Explanation:** Avoiding is acceptable for low-impact issues where the effort to resolve exceeds the benefit.

## Question 19
A PM is working in a matrix organization. A functional manager pulls a resource off the project without notice. What is the PM's first step?
- [ ] Replace the resource with a contractor.
- [ ] Complain to the sponsor.
- [x] Meet with the functional manager to understand the reason and discuss project impact.
- [ ] Update the risk register and do nothing else.
**Explanation:** Direct communication to resolve the conflict and understand the constraint is the first step.

## Question 20
A team's ground rules were established but are now being ignored. This has led to several small conflicts. What should the PM do?
- [ ] Discipline the team members.
- [ ] Write new ground rules and distribute them.
- [x] Facilitate a team meeting to review and recommit to the ground rules.
- [ ] Ignore it as it is a natural part of team evolution.
**Explanation:** Ground rules are a team contract; the team must be involved in reviewing and enforcing them.

## Question 21
In a hybrid project, the agile development team and the predictive marketing team are at odds over delivery dates. The PM facilitates a session to align the two schedules. What is this?
- [ ] Forcing
- [ ] Withdrawal
- [x] Problem Solving
- [ ] Smoothing
**Explanation:** Aligning different perspectives to meet project goals is problem solving/collaboration.

## Question 22
A project manager uses their position of power to end an argument about a technical detail. What is the likely result?
- [ ] A win-win outcome.
- [x] A win-lose outcome and potential resentment.
- [ ] A long-term technical solution.
- [ ] Increased team cohesion.
**Explanation:** Forcing creates a winner and a loser, which often damages morale.

## Question 23
A customer wants a change that the lead engineer says is impossible. The PM brings them together to look at the data and requirements. What are they doing?
- [x] Collaborating to find a solution.
- [ ] Compromising on the scope.
- [ ] Avoiding a difficult conversation.
- [ ] Smoothing over the customer's anger.
**Explanation:** Bringing parties together to look at facts and find a way forward is collaboration.

## Question 24
Which of the following is an example of a "Project Manager as a Facilitator" in conflict?
- [ ] Telling the team how to work.
- [x] Helping the team identify the pros and cons of different options.
- [ ] Assigning blame when a deadline is missed.
- [ ] Making all technical decisions.
**Explanation:** Facilitation involves guiding the process of decision-making without dictating the outcome.

## Question 25
Conflict management is part of which process group?
- [ ] Planning
- [x] Executing
- [ ] Closing
- [ ] Initiating
**Explanation:** Managing conflict occurs during the "Manage Team" process in the Executing phase.

## Question 26
A team member is upset because they feel their contributions are not recognized. The PM listens and validates their feelings. This is an application of:
- [ ] Technical skills
- [x] Emotional Intelligence
- [ ] Administrative skills
- [ ] Forcing
**Explanation:** Recognizing and responding to the emotions of others is a core part of Emotional Intelligence in conflict.

## Question 27
Two team members have a personality clash that is disrupting the office. The PM moves one to a different floor. This is:
- [ ] Problem Solving
- [x] Withdrawal/Avoidance
- [ ] Compromise
- [ ] Collaboration
**Explanation:** Physically separating people to avoid the conflict is a form of withdrawal/avoidance.

## Question 28
Which conflict resolution technique is most likely to be used when a project manager has little time and needs the team to just move forward?
- [ ] Collaboration
- [x] Forcing
- [ ] Problem Solving
- [ ] Negotiating
**Explanation:** Forcing is often used in emergencies when a quick decision is required.

## Question 29
A PM encourages the team to handle their own conflicts but steps in when the conflict becomes personal or destructive. This PM is practicing:
- [ ] Laissez-faire leadership
- [x] Servant leadership
- [ ] Dictatorial leadership
- [ ] Transactional leadership
**Explanation:** Servant leaders empower teams to resolve issues but provide a safety net when needed.

## Question 30
A project manager is dealing with a conflict between the vendor and the internal team regarding the definition of a "bug." The PM refers to the Service Level Agreement (SLA). This is:
- [ ] Smoothing
- [ ] Avoiding
- [ ] Forcing
- [x] Problem Solving/Collaboration (using a shared fact base)
**Explanation:** Using the contract or SLA as a "single source of truth" helps resolve the conflict based on objective criteria.
