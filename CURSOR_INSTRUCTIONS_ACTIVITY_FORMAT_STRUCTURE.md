# CURSOR INSTRUCTIONS: Activity Session Format & Structure

## Overview

This document defines the **standard format and structure** for all 6 activities across the 35 PMP Learn Hub tasks. Use this as the template for building consistent, high-quality learning activities.

---

## THE 6-ACTIVITY MODEL

Every task in the Learn Hub follows the same 6-activity structure:

| # | Activity Type | Purpose | Format Type |
|---|---------------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | PM Simulator | Apply knowledge to realistic scenarios | Scenario-based decision making |
| 2 | Lightning Round | Build rapid pattern recognition | Quick-fire Q&A |
| 3 | Document Detective | Analyze project artifacts | Artifact analysis |
| 4 | Timeline Reconstructor | Understand sequences and progression | Event sequencing |
| 5 | Relationship Matcher | Connect concepts to situations | Matching exercise |
| 6 | Empathy Exercise | See from other perspectives | Perspective-taking |

# ACTIVITY 1: PM SIMULATOR

## Purpose
Practice applying task knowledge to realistic project scenarios through decision-making exercises.

## Standard Format

```markdown
← Back
[Task Name]
PM Simulator

Interactive Scenario-Based Learning

Welcome to the PM Simulator for [Task Name]. This tool presents realistic project scenarios where you must [task-specific decision type]. Each scenario tests your ability to apply [primary framework(s)] in context.

---

## How to Use This Simulator

1. **Read each scenario carefully** - Note [relevant details for this task]
2. **Diagnose the situation** - [Task-specific diagnosis prompt]
3. **Select your approach** - Choose the most appropriate response
4. **Review feedback** - Understand why your choice was right or wrong
5. **Learn and improve** - Apply insights to real situations

---

## Scenario 1: [Descriptive Title]

### Situation

[2-4 paragraph scenario setup including:]
- Context (project type, team composition, timeline)
- Specific situation/problem
- Key details that inform the decision
- Enough ambiguity to make it realistic

[Optional: Character names, specific data, history]

**What should you do [FIRST/NEXT/in this situation]?**

A) [Option A - plausible but wrong]
B) [Option B - plausible but wrong]
C) [Option C - correct answer]
D) [Option D - plausible but wrong]
E) [Option E - if needed]

### Correct Answer: [Letter]

**Why [Letter] is correct:**
[2-3 sentences explaining the principle and why this is the best choice]

**Why others are wrong:**
- **A**: [Why this is wrong - 1 sentence]
- **B**: [Why this is wrong - 1 sentence]
- **D**: [Why this is wrong - 1 sentence]
- **E**: [Why this is wrong - 1 sentence]

**Key Principle:** [One-sentence takeaway in italics]

---

[REPEAT FOR 5-8 SCENARIOS]

---

## Summary: Key Takeaways

| Situation | Right Approach |
|-----------|----------------|
| [Situation type 1] | [Correct approach] |
| [Situation type 2] | [Correct approach] |
| [Situation type 3] | [Correct approach] |
| [Situation type 4] | [Correct approach] |
```

## PM Simulator Requirements

- **5-8 scenarios** per task
- **4-5 answer options** per scenario
- **One clearly correct answer** (not "it depends")
- **Plausible wrong answers** (common mistakes, not obviously wrong)
- **Scenario length**: 100-200 words setup
- **Analysis length**: 50-100 words per scenario
- **Covers all ECO enablers** for the task
- **Realistic project contexts** (agile, waterfall, hybrid)

# ACTIVITY 2: LIGHTNING ROUND

## Purpose
Build rapid pattern recognition through quick-fire questions that test instant recall and recognition.

## Standard Format

```markdown
← Back
[Task Name]
Lightning Round

Rapid-Fire [Task-Specific Recognition Type]

Welcome to the Lightning Round! You'll see brief scenarios and must quickly identify the correct [task-specific element]. This builds your rapid recognition of [Task Name] patterns.

---

## Instructions

- Read each quick scenario
- Select your answer quickly (exam time pressure!)
- Review the explanation
- Look for pattern recognition

---

## Round 1: [Category Name]

**Q1:** [Brief scenario - 1-2 sentences max]
**[Question prompt]**
A) [Option A]
B) [Option B]
C) [Option C]

**Answer: [Letter]** - [1 sentence explanation]

---

**Q2:** [Brief scenario]
**[Question prompt]**
A) [Option A]
B) [Option B]
C) [Option C]

**Answer: [Letter]** - [1 sentence explanation]

---

[CONTINUE Q3-Q5 for Round 1]

---

## Round 2: [Category Name]

[Q6-Q10]

---

## Round 3: [Category Name]

[Q11-Q15]

---

## Round 4: [Category Name]

[Q16-Q20]

---

## Round 5: Speed Round - [Special Focus]

[Q21-Q25 - fastest format, most pressure]

---

## Final Score Interpretation

- **23-25 correct**: Expert level—[positive description]
- **18-22 correct**: Strong foundation—review missed areas
- **13-17 correct**: Good start—study the Deep Dive
- **Below 13**: Revisit fundamentals—focus on [key concepts]
```

## Lightning Round Requirements

- **25 questions total** organized in 5 rounds of 5
- **Brief scenarios**: 1-2 sentences max
- **3 answer options** (A, B, C) - keeps it fast
- **1-sentence explanations** only
- **Rounds organized by category** (different aspects of the task)
- **Final round is "speed round"** with fastest format
- **Score interpretation** at the end
- **Builds pattern recognition** for exam

# ACTIVITY 4: TIMELINE RECONSTRUCTOR

## Purpose
Understand how situations evolve over time by sequencing scrambled events and identifying patterns and interventions.

## Standard Format

```markdown
← Back
[Task Name]
Timeline Reconstructor

Sequence a [Context] Journey Through [Time Period]

Welcome to Timeline Reconstructor! In this activity, you'll arrange scrambled events from a [time period] [context] into chronological order, identify [task-specific patterns], and analyze how PM interventions affected outcomes.

---

## The Scenario: [Character/Team]'s [Journey Type]

**Context:** [2-3 sentences setting up the scenario]

**Team/Setting:** [Brief description]
**Timeframe:** [e.g., 12 months, 8 sprints, etc.]
**PM Focus:** [What the PM is learning/doing]

---

## SCRAMBLED EVENTS

Arrange these [12-15] events in chronological order:

---

**Event A:**
> [Event description - 2-4 sentences with specific details]

**Event B:**
> [Event description]

**Event C:**
> [Event description]

[Continue through all events - typically 12-15 total]

---

## CORRECT CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER

**[Time Period 1]:**
**[Letter]** → [Brief event description]

**[Time Period 2]:**
**[Letter]** → [Brief event description]

[Continue through all time periods]

---

## TIMELINE ANALYSIS

### Phase 1: [Phase Name] ([Time Period])

**Events:** [Letters in sequence]

**Pattern:** [What was happening during this phase]

**PM Actions (Good or Bad):**
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]

**Lesson:** *[One-sentence lesson in italics]*

---

### Phase 2: [Phase Name] ([Time Period])

[Same structure]

---

### Phase 3: [Phase Name] ([Time Period])

[Same structure]

---

### The Turning Point

**[Specific Event]** was the critical moment. [Explanation of why and what could have happened differently]

---

## REFLECTION QUESTIONS

1. **[Question about prevention]**
   - [Discussion points]

2. **[Question about single biggest change]**
   - [Discussion points]

3. **[Question connecting to task framework]**
   - [Discussion points]

---

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

| Phase | What Happened | What Should Have Happened |
|-------|---------------|---------------------------|
| [Phase 1] | [Description] | [Better approach] |
| [Phase 2] | [Description] | [Better approach] |
| [Phase 3] | [Description] | [Better approach] |
```

## Timeline Reconstructor Requirements

- **12-15 scrambled events**
- **Clear time markers** (months, sprints, weeks)
- **3-4 distinct phases** in the story
- **One turning point** that could have gone either way
- **Story arc**: Typically problem → decline → intervention → recovery OR success → complacency → warning signs → crisis
- **PM learning journey** embedded in the story
- **Reflection questions** that connect to task frameworks
- **Summary table** showing phases and lessons

### Timeline Story Types by Task

| Task Type | Good Story Arcs |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Lead a Team | Team through Tuckman stages with regression |
| Support Performance | High performer becoming disengaged → recovery |
| Manage Conflict | Conflict escalating → resolution |
| Stakeholder | Stakeholder relationship deteriorating → rebuilding |
| Risk | Risks materializing due to ignored warnings |
