## Round 1: Team Stage Recognition

### Question 1
Team members are polite but ask many questions about roles and processes. Productivity is low as everyone is getting oriented.

**What stage is this team in?**
A) Storming
B) Forming
C) Norming
D) Performing

<details>
<summary>Click for Answer</summary>

**Answer: B) Forming**

**Why:** 
- Polite behavior = Forming characteristic
- Questions about roles/processes = need for structure
- Low productivity during orientation = Forming pattern
- Getting oriented = beginning stage

**Key Indicator:** "Polite" is the giveaway - Forming teams are tentative and polite. Storming teams are not polite!
</details>

## Round 4: Common Mistakes

### Question 17
PM lets new team self-organize immediately without providing structure. What's the problem?

**Answer:**
A) Nothing, empowerment is good
B) Team can't self-organize in Forming stage
C) Should use Pacesetting instead
D) Should use Affiliative instead

<details>
<summary>Click for Answer</summary>

**Answer: B) Team can't self-organize in Forming stage**

**Why:**
- Forming teams need structure
- Can't self-organize without foundation
- Creates confusion and anxiety
- Appropriate for Performing, not Forming

**Common Mistake:** Delegating too early with immature teams.
</details>

---

### Question 18
PM forces decision to quickly end team argument. What's the problem?

**Answer:**
A) Nothing, decisiveness is good
B) Suppresses Storming, prolongs conflict
C) Should use Democratic instead
D) Should use Affiliative instead

<details>
<summary>Click for Answer</summary>

**Answer: B) Suppresses Storming, prolongs conflict**

**Why:**
- Storming conflict needs facilitation
- Forcing decision drives conflict underground
- Team doesn't learn to resolve issues
- Prolongs Storming stage

**Common Mistake:** Using Commanding to suppress Storming stage conflict.
</details>

---

### Question 22
Two senior team members disagree on architecture. Both have valid points. What should PM do FIRST?

**Answer:**
A) Decide yourself immediately
B) Let them work it out alone
C) Facilitate structured discussion
D) Side with one approach

<details>
<summary>Click for Answer</summary>

**Answer: C) Facilitate structured discussion**

**Why:**
- Storming behavior (power struggle)
- Both have expertise (need their input)
- PM facilitates, doesn't dictate
- Coaching style appropriate

**Key Point:** Facilitate technical debates, don't force or avoid.

- Engaged = taking ownership
- PM supports, doesn't take over
- Delegating style appropriate

**Key Point:** Don't regress to directive style with Performing teams facing challenges.
</details>

### Question 24
Team member shares personal crisis affecting work. What should PM do FIRST?

**Answer:**
A) Reassign their work immediately
B) Express empathy, ask what they need
C) Maintain professional distance
D) Tell them to take time off

<details>
<summary>Click for Answer</summary>

**Why:**
- Lead with empathy (emotional intelligence)
- Ask what THEY need (respect autonomy)
- Offer support and flexibility
- Affiliative style appropriate

**Key Point:** Don't make decisions for people in crisis - ask what they need.
</details>

## Quick Reference Card

### Tuckman's Stages (Memorize This)

**Forming:** Polite, uncertain, dependent → Provide structure
**Storming:** Conflict, power struggles → Facilitate, coach
**Norming:** Cooperation, "we" language → Involve, empower
**Performing:** Autonomous, excellent → Delegate, support
**Adjourning:** Reflective, transitioning → Celebrate, capture lessons

### Leadership Styles (Memorize This)

**Commanding:** "Do what I say" → Emergency only → Most negative impact
**Authoritative:** "Come with me" → New direction → Most positive impact
**Affiliative:** "People first" → After stress → Builds harmony
**Democratic:** "What do you think?" → Complex decisions → Need buy-in
**Pacesetting:** "Do as I do" → Short bursts → Can damage if overused
**Coaching:** "Try this" → Development → Long-term capability

### Stage → Style Matching (Memorize This)

- Forming → Authoritative/Commanding
- Storming → Coaching/Affiliative
- Norming → Democratic
- Performing → Delegating
- Adjourning → Coaching/Affiliative

### Servant Leadership (Remember This)

- Remove obstacles
- Empower team
- Give credit to team
- Support over direct
- Team success over personal recognition
