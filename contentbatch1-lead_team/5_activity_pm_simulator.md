# Activity: PM Simulator Lightning Round

## Activity Overview

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes  
**Format:** Individual → Small Group Discussion  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply Tuckman's model to diagnose team development stages
- Select appropriate leadership styles for different situations
- Practice rapid decision-making under pressure
- Recognize common team leadership challenges

---

## Instructions

You'll be presented with 10 realistic project scenarios. For each scenario:

1. **Diagnose** the team development stage (Forming/Storming/Norming/Performing/Adjourning)
2. **Select** the most appropriate leadership style (Commanding/Authoritative/Affiliative/Democratic/Pacesetting/Coaching)
3. **Explain** your reasoning in 1-2 sentences

Work through scenarios individually first (10 minutes), then discuss your choices in small groups (15 minutes).

## Answer Key (For Facilitator)

### Scenario 1: New Project Kickoff
- **Stage:** Forming
- **Style:** Commanding → Authoritative (provide structure, then inspire with vision)
- **Reasoning:** Team needs clear direction and roles established. Set structure first, then articulate compelling vision to build enthusiasm.

### Scenario 2: Power Struggle
- **Stage:** Storming
- **Style:** Coaching (with some Affiliative)
- **Reasoning:** Team is experiencing natural power struggles. Need to facilitate conflict resolution while maintaining relationships. Coach individuals through disagreements.

### Scenario 3: Finding Their Rhythm
- **Stage:** Norming
- **Style:** Participative/Democratic (with Coaching)
- **Reasoning:** Team is ready for more involvement in decisions. Step back strategically and facilitate collaboration while continuing to develop capabilities.

### Scenario 4: Peak Performance
- **Stage:** Performing
- **Style:** Delegating (minimal intervention)
- **Reasoning:** Team is self-organizing effectively. Trust them, remove obstacles, and focus on strategic issues. Don't micromanage success.

### Scenario 5: The Crisis
- **Stage:** Could be any stage, but crisis requires immediate response
- **Style:** Commanding
- **Reasoning:** Emergency requires quick, decisive action. This is the rare appropriate use of directive leadership. Provide clear instructions and coordinate response.

### Scenario 6: Burned Out Team
- **Stage:** Could regress to Storming if not addressed
- **Style:** Affiliative (people first)
- **Reasoning:** Team needs emotional support and recovery. Prioritize relationships and well-being. Show care and provide relief before pushing for performance.

### Scenario 7: Technical Decision
- **Stage:** Norming or Performing (team is capable)
- **Style:** Democratic
- **Reasoning:** Complex decision requiring expert input and strong buy-in. Team has expertise. Involve them in decision-making process.

### Scenario 8: Underperformer
- **Stage:** Team stage varies, but individual needs directive approach
- **Style:** Commanding (for Alex specifically)
- **Reasoning:** Coaching has failed. Clear expectations and accountability are needed. This is individual performance management, not general team leadership.

### Scenario 9: Career Development
- **Stage:** Performing (stable team with development opportunity)
- **Style:** Coaching
- **Reasoning:** Perfect situation for development-focused leadership. Provide stretch assignments, feedback, and guidance to build capabilities.

### Scenario 10: The Ending
- **Stage:** Adjourning
- **Style:** Coaching/Affiliative
- **Reasoning:** Team needs support through transition. Acknowledge emotions, celebrate success, capture lessons learned, and help with next steps.

## Discussion Questions

After completing scenarios, discuss:

1. **Which scenarios were most challenging to diagnose?** Why?

2. **Did you notice any patterns** in which stages appeared most frequently?

3. **How comfortable are you** with using different leadership styles?

4. **What surprised you** about the recommended approaches?

5. **Can you think of similar situations** from your own project experience?

6. **Which leadership style** do you need to develop further?

7. **How would you handle** scenarios where you disagreed with the "answer key"?

8. **What would happen** if you used the wrong leadership style in these situations?

## Facilitator Notes

**Time Management:**
- Individual work: 10 minutes (1 minute per scenario)
- Small group discussion: 15 minutes
- Large group debrief: 10 minutes
- Total: 35 minutes

**Setup:**
- Print scenarios or provide digitally
- Create small groups of 3-4 people
- Have answer key ready but don't share until after discussion

**Facilitation Tips:**
- Emphasize there may be multiple valid approaches
- Focus on reasoning, not just "right" answers
- Encourage debate and different perspectives
- Connect to participants' real experiences
- Note that situations often require style combinations

**Common Discussion Points:**
- Situations rarely fit perfectly into one category
- Style transitions and combinations are important
- Cultural factors may influence appropriate styles
- Virtual teams may require adaptations
- Organizational context matters

**Adaptations:**
- **Beginner level:** Provide stage/style options for each scenario
- **Advanced level:** Add complications (budget cuts, executive pressure, etc.)
- **Virtual delivery:** Use breakout rooms for small group discussion
- **Self-paced:** Provide answer key with detailed explanations
